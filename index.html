About

A research agenda shaped by real soundscapes, not idealized benchmarks.

My work is motivated by everyday acoustic environments in Ghana: transport stations, market speech, generators during power cuts, and multilingual public spaces where noise, overlap, and device variation are the norm.

I am interested in building datasets, evaluation pipelines, and machine learning systems that remain useful when accent, channel, hardware, and environment shift away from the clean conditions typical of many benchmarks.

Research interests: robust speech recognition, low-resource and multilingual speech technology, acoustic event understanding, self-supervised learning, federated learning, and practical evaluation under distribution shift.

Research themes

Questions I keep returning to

I care less about headline accuracy in isolation and more about whether systems remain trustworthy when the setting becomes messy, noisy, sparse, multilingual, or unevenly distributed.

Robust ASR under shift

How do speech systems behave when SNR, speaker accent, recording device, and conversational context drift from training conditions?

Fairness, privacy, and non-IID learning

I am interested in federated and privacy-aware settings where overall gains can hide uneven performance across groups or clients.

Low-resource speech infrastructure

Data curation, annotation quality, manifest design, and evaluation still matter profoundly in under-represented languages and settings.

Audio intelligence in realistic scenes

Environmental sound recognition, speech enhancement, and compact edge models are most useful when grounded in real auditory scenes.
