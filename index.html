About

A research agenda grounded in real acoustic environments.

My work is motivated by everyday Ghanaian soundscapes: crowded transport stations, market speech, generators during power cuts, and multilingual public environments. I am interested in designing systems that stay reliable when noise, accent, device, and deployment context shift.

My broader goal is to build reusable datasets, careful evaluation recipes, and practical tools for speech and audio research that better reflect the realities of African and other low-resource settings.

Research interests: robust speech and audio modeling, self-supervised and data-efficient learning, privacy-preserving and federated speech technologies, auditory scene analysis, and evaluation under domain shift.

Research

What I work on

A through-line across my projects is that aggregate performance is not enough. I care about worst-case conditions, group-wise robustness, and deployable systems.

Robust ASR under domain shift

Speech systems should degrade gracefully as SNR, environment, accent, and hardware change—not only on clean benchmarks.

Fairness and privacy

I study learning settings such as federated and privacy-aware training, with attention to worst-group risk and non-IID behavior.

Audio perception in realistic scenes

I’m interested in auditory scene analysis, low-resource acoustic intelligence, and compact models for embedded assistive systems.

Selected projects

Research and engineering work

Dataset · Robust audio

Ghana Soundscapes Benchmark

Curated an environmental audio dataset from urban Ghana and built integrity-checked manifests with speaker- and location-disjoint splits. Used CNN baselines and augmentation to study robustness under noise, sparsity, and device shift.

View repository

ASR · Federated learning

FedSpeech-Ghana

Fine-tuned wav2vec 2.0 and simulated federated learning with Ghanaian English accents as non-IID clients, tracking global and per-accent WER to expose optimization and fairness trade-offs.

View repository

Low-resource speech

SESA: Asante Twi ↔ English Speech

Contributed to Whisper fine-tuning for Twi and code-switched speech, with emphasis on manifests, normalization, evaluation scripts, and error analysis for rare words and code switching.

Ongoing research collaboration

Embedded audio · Assistive tech

Real-time models for hearing technologies

Trained compact convolutional, recurrent, and temporal models for sound classification and speech enhancement on low-power systems, balancing latency, memory, and intelligibility.
