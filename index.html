About

A research direction grounded in real acoustic environments.

My work is motivated by everyday public soundscapes in Ghana: transport stations, markets, generator-dominated neighborhoods, and multilingual environments where clean benchmark assumptions often break down.

I am interested in building datasets, evaluation pipelines, and learning systems that remain useful when accent, hardware, recording conditions, and noise shift away from curated laboratory settings.

Research interests: robust speech and audio modeling, self-supervised and data-efficient learning, privacy-preserving and federated speech technologies, acoustic intelligence, and evaluation under domain shift.

Research themes

Questions I keep coming back to

I care less about a single aggregate score and more about whether systems stay reliable when the setting becomes noisy, sparse, multilingual, or unevenly distributed.

Robust ASR under domain shift

How should speech models behave when SNR, accent, hardware, or conversational context drift away from training conditions?

Fairness and privacy

I study settings where overall performance can look stable while smaller groups or clients degrade quietly in the background.

Low-resource speech infrastructure

Careful manifests, annotation quality, split design, and evaluation recipes still matter profoundly in under-represented settings.

Audio intelligence in realistic scenes

Environmental sound recognition and compact audio models are most useful when built around the complexity of real auditory scenes.

Selected work

Research and engineering projects

Dataset · Environmental audio

Ghana Soundscapes Benchmark

Curated an environmental audio dataset from urban Ghana, built integrity-checked manifests with speaker and location disjoint splits, and trained CNN baselines with augmentation to study robustness under noise, sparsity, and device shift.

Dataset design Validation analysis Real-world acoustic scenes

ASR · Federated learning

FedSpeech-Ghana

Fine-tuned wav2vec 2.0 and simulated FedAvg with Ghanaian English accents as non-IID clients, tracking global and per-accent WER to study fairness and optimization trade-offs.

Low-resource speech

SESA: Asante Twi ↔ English speech

Contributed to Whisper fine-tuning for Twi and code-switched speech, focusing on manifests, normalization, evaluation scripts, and error analysis for low-resource speech technology.

Embedded audio

Real-time robust speech/audio on embedded devices

Trained compact models for sound classification and speech enhancement on a low-power system-on-chip, profiling latency, memory, and evaluation under practical deployment constraints.

Applied ML

Breast cancer detection web application

Built a VGG19-based mammogram classifier and deployed an end-to-end web application using a FastAPI backend and a React/Tailwind frontend.
